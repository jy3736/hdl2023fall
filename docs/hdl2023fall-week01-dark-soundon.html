Course Overview

Course Description

This course provides a thorough exploration of digital systems and Hardware Description Languages (HDLs) for effective design. With a structured curriculum, students delve into HDL fundamentals, particularly focusing on Verilog. The course starts by introducing digital systems and the role of HDLs in design. Practical skills are honed through hands-on Verilog exercises, progressing from syntax to crafting logic circuits. Combinational Logic Design covers gates, multiplexers, decoders, and encoders, with reinforced concepts through testbench development. Sequential Logic Design follows, introducing flip-flops, latches, registers, counters, and Finite State Machines (FSMs) using Verilog. The course includes a Midterm Exam and explores Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and University Social Responsibility (USR) through Tabletop Gaming. Synthesis, on-board testing, memory, storage, and System-Level Design are also covered. The course concludes with a focus on Final Project Validation, Refinement, and the culminating Project/Exam.
